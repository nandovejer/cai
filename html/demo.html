Why?

Classless, less mess

CAI breaks away from traditional CSS frameworks with its classless design. This approach simplifies your HTML, reducing clutter and enhancing readability, while still offering a sleek, modern aesthetic.

Accessible is inevitable

Built with the WAI-ARIA guidelines at its core, CAI ensures that every user, regardless of their abilities, enjoys a seamless web experience. Our framework is a testament to inclusivity in the digital realm.

Modular, this is spectacular!

Each module is crafted to work independently or in harmony with other. This minimal, classless framework is the perfect ally for small to medium-sized projects where accessibility isn’t just a feature, but a necessity.

99% without js, dialog and polifills are guilty about this

CAI stands proud with a 99% JavaScript-free environment.This approach ensures a smoother, faster, and more secure experience for both developers and end-users. By minimizing reliance on JavaScript, CAI guarantees al4

RTL Friendly, Seamlessly Dreamly

Embracing global diversity, CAI is thoughtfully designed to be RTL (Right-to-Left) friendly, ensuring seamless integration for languages that flow from right to left. This feature makes CAI a dream come true for multilingual websites, providing a hassle-free experience in creating layouts that cater to a wider audience.

Create a Theme Like a King

The framework’s theming capabilities are robust yet simple, allowing you to craft custom themes with ease. Whether it’s adjusting color schemes, typography, or layout, CAI gives you the royal tools to dictate every aspect of your website’s appearance. Unleash your creativity and rule your digital kingdom, making your website not just a space but an extension of your unique brand and style.

Forms

All form elements are fully responsive in pure semantic HTML, allowing forms to scale gracefully across devices and viewports.

Inputs are width: 100%; by default. You can use .grid inside a form.

All native form elements are fully customizable and themeable with CSS variables.

Modal

The classic modal element with graceful spacings across devices and viewports.

Modals are built with <dialog> as a wrapper and <article> as a content container.

Inside <header>, <a class="close"> is defined to float: right; allowing a close icon to be top aligned with a title.

Modal title

Nunc nec ligula a tortor sollicitudin dictum in vel enim. Quisque facilisis turpis vel eros dictum aliquam et nec turpis. Sed eleifend a dui nec ullamcorper. Praesent vehicula lacus ac justo accumsan ullamcorper.

 
<dialog open>
        <article>
          <header>
            <a href="#close" aria-label="Close" class="close"></a>
            Modal title
          </header>
          <p>
            Nunc nec ligula a tortor sollicitudin dictum in vel enim. 
            Quisque facilisis turpis vel eros dictum aliquam et nec turpis. 
            Sed eleifend a dui nec ullamcorper. 
            Praesent vehicula lacus ac justo accumsan ullamcorper.
          </p>
        </article>
      </dialog>
